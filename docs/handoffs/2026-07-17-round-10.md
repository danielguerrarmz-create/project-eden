## Why it went this way

**Item 0.** Round 9 measured its ratios honestly — all five were exact. But `bbebe11` **never
`git add`ed the five .webp files**. It compiled, and it ran clean on the author's machine because the
files sat in his working tree; every one would have 404'd for everybody else and in the deployed
build. Nothing could catch it: a missing asset is not a type error and the dev server serves an
untracked file happily. `timeline-photos.test.ts` now asks `git ls-files` — *what does a stranger get
when they clone* — which is the question that makes it catchable.

**Two tests were already failing at `bbebe11`.** Not from this round. The "354, do not regress"
baseline was broken when round 10 inherited it; the session-close doc said "compiles clean at this
SHA", which was true and is not the same claim. Both had pinned a MEASUREMENT as a LAW:

- *"every gap on a side is equal, and far wider than packSide ever left"* never checked that the gaps
  were equal — the actual thing `spreadSide` exists to guarantee. It checked `> 100`, a number from
  the content on the page the day it was written. Five photographs divided the same slack into more
  gaps, the lane evened out at 99.61, and it failed while the lane did exactly what it promises.
- *"not one branch point lands on a project plate"* asserted more than the design promises. Its own
  comment conceded "the algorithm does not promise this on its own" and then asserted it anyway.

**Item 2 was twice the bug.** The base offset was real (`CARD_LINE = 0.52`; growth started at
50.6–51.3%, exactly Daniel's "a little before the halfway mark"). Underneath it: the sub-branches
lagged **55 units per order** against a tree that space colonization grows **21 deep** = **1155 units**
of stagger against a span of 175. Measured: **195 of 195 runs unfinished at halfway; 64 (33%) still
growing after they had scrolled off the top of the screen.** Nobody chose 1155 — it fell out of a
per-order rate meeting an EMERGENT depth, so the ornament was deciding how long the page's reveal
took.

**Item 6 was not an effect to tone down.** `organAt` was keyed to a growth that saturates at 1 while
asking for `t + LAG + FADE`, so every organ past t=0.66 was unreachable. **87 of 218 (40%) could never
fully open; 30 (14%) were painted into the bitmap and masked out forever.** "It reads half-off" was
literal. There is no de-render (`camY` is monotonic); a flower blooming partway and stalling is what
reads as one.

## The lesson this round keeps repeating

**A confident comment is not a measurement, and this page writes very confident comments.** Three
separate ones were wrong about things nobody had measured:

- `reveal.ts`: "THE TIMELINE DOES NOT USE THIS AND MUST NOT. Its branches are short... completes at
  ~38% of the frame, well inside." True of order 0 and only order 0 — 38 of 195 runs. It reasoned
  about the span and forgot the lag stacked on top of it.
- `revealProps`: "both terminals sit off-frame and the line runs edge to edge." True of the viewBox,
  false of the screen.
- The contract test: "SUB_PLATE_PAD (18) being wider than SUB_SEGMENT (9)" holds across an EDGE and
  says nothing about a CORNER, which is where it actually broke.

The common shape: each reasoned correctly about one case and generalised. **The files most worth
distrusting are the ones that explain themselves best**, because the prose is what stops the next
person measuring. Every claim above was checked by sabotage before it was believed — including four
of my own, three of which were wrong:

- a ratio tolerance of 5e-3 that passed a guessed `1.5` against a measured `1.5009`;
- an off-screen test that searched downward from the frame's edge and so could never see off-screen;
- a bio guard keyed on `p.id`, **a field that does not exist**, so every lookup returned undefined,
  every project was skipped, and it passed green while checking nothing — in the file whose entire
  purpose is catching things that check nothing;
- and a comment claiming node's globals were contained when they were not.
