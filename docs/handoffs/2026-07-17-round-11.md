## Why / how

**Item 5 — the precompute cannot change a pixel, and that is the point.** The stagger was NOT the
intro text (1.6%); it was `colonize()` run synchronously during render. `subBranchPolylines()` takes
no args, `SUB_SEED` is hardcoded, and it reads no DOM, so it is byte-identical every load. It is baked
to `subBranches.generated.json` and read at render; `subBranches.generated.test.ts` is BOTH the
generator (`GEN=1 vitest run subBranches.generated`) and a drift guard that fails if the seed or params
change without a regen. Measured at 4× throttle: `longTaskMs` 9661 → 1249, `ornamentReadyMs`
13258 → 4799; scrollFps unchanged (35), confirming the frozen intro — not scroll — was the cost.

**Item 8 — scale is decoupled from stroke, so the enlargement is free.** Round 10 made the mark's
circles stroke at a literal `SPINE_W`, not `MARK_STROKE * k`, so bumping `MARK_K` grows the mark and
its whole finale (tail, ring, wordmark position, track height all hang off it) WITHOUT touching the
ink. Verified on the real render: a mark circle strokes 2.2px == the spine's 2.2px. The mark clears
the frame at 1440×900, ×720, and down to ×640 (10.6px margin at the wordmark) — no clip, and the frame
was NOT resized to achieve it. Item-3 organs still read as grown foliage on the ring. The mark-size
test now asserts Daniel's ~1.4x band (not a hardcoded pixel — that was item 1a's trap) plus the
`SPINE_W` decoupling. `qa/hero-lockup.mjs` was fixed: it reads `[data-timeline-viewport]` (the true
camera window, not the item-1b-bled svg — the whole reason it was red while the lockup was
pixel-perfect), re-derives the camY want honestly (~4933.7, was a stale 3806.1), and adds a
mark-stroke invariant.

**Item 2 — wait for the THING, not the clock.** The cue shows only when the mark is fully wound
(`windW > 0.9 && p >= PIN_FRAC` — true only within a few percent of the pin, since `windW` falls the
instant the unravel begins) AND the reader has gone idle. Stillness alone never gates it, so it cannot
mistake "not started" for "finished". Fades on the next scroll, no bounce, `pointer-events-none`, not
rendered in reduced motion. **Its chevron is CSS, not `<svg>`, on purpose** (see the trap below).

**Item 7 — Daniel ruled (A): CLOSE THE BOWER.** The arms' tails used to trail off as bare stems ~1157px
apart; they now curl in and MEET at the content centre with a 0px join, enclosing the founders — a
bower is a leafy enclosure. The meeting is a CONNECTION (a fact): both arms end on `L.trunkX` exactly.
The curl SHAPE is by eye. `parenthesis.ts` no longer describes the old open "closing bracket, not two
rails" form — that intent is dead and the comments say so. The floating detached bloom was the reveal
mask indexing the arm's 6px Catmull-Rom sampling while the painter indexes its own even 4px resample;
`FounderParenthesis.marks` now uses the exported `resample` so disc and organ are one point.

## The trap this round produced: an `<svg>` in the wrong subtree

The item-2 cue first used an `<svg width="18">` for its chevron, placed inside the sticky finale row.
`FounderParenthesis` (and three qa harnesses) reach the finale camera with
`querySelector('[data-timeline-track] svg')`, which returns the FIRST svg — now the chevron. The
founders' trunk width is `SPINE_W * (tr.width / TIMELINE_W)`, so an 18px "timeline svg" made it
`2.2 * 18/1200 = 0.033px` and the **entire bower went invisible in motion** while rendering perfectly
in reduced. Same class as CLAUDE.md's `data-timeline-camera` note. Fixed by drawing the chevron as a
rotated bordered box — the same glyph with nothing for that selector to grab. `qa/hero-lockup.mjs`
survived only because its assertions had already moved off the svg onto `[data-timeline-viewport]`.
