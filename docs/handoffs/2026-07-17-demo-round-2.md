## What shipped

### Part 1 — the built kit (`Folly.tsx`, `connectors.ts`)

1. **Eased-arris members.** `prismsToGeometry` now chamfers the 4 long running
   edges of each member so the box section reads as an octagon, capped, the way
   a shop breaks the arris on planed stock. The two end-cap (cut) faces are NOT
   chamfered — those are the honest joint. Render bevel only; moves no BOM line,
   no `config.ts` number. Depth by stock (constants at the call site): C24
   struts/feet 4 mm, LVL blanks/lamella 3 mm. Triangle count per member 12 → 28
   as the spec worked out; that is GPU buffer, not bundle.
2. **The dome nut** (`JOINTS.hub.boltSpec` names it, Folly drew nothing): one
   flat cylinder cap per bolt, threaded on the outer face. Ø22 × 12 mm, a
   proportion off the 12 mm bolt, not a spec dimension.
3. **The ground screw** (`FOUNDATION.groundScrewSpec`): a Ø76 collar (real Ø)
   rising a visible ~60 mm above each base plate. Visible height is a proportion,
   tuned DOWN from Sai's ~100 mm so it does not fight the foot timber on the same
   node. Both are eyeball-tunable named constants.
4. **Two-tone steel by role.** Structural steel keeps the tuned galvanized tone
   (`#aab0b4`); fasteners (bolts, the new nut caps, the new screw stubs) go dark
   (`#3a382f`) so a bolt reads recessed against the plate it holds. `connectors.ts`
   tags each cylinder `structural | fastener` (`cylRoles`, parallel to
   `cylinders`, pushed alongside each cylinder because a node emits both kinds);
   `Folly` drives it through `instanceColor` on the one cylinder mesh.

### Part 2 — the dynamic figure (`priceCopy.ts`, `useCountUp.ts`, `DrawPage.tsx`)

`commissionDemoFigureGBP` moves the demo figure off the anchor bake (14.3 m², 194
pieces, clematis → exactly £150,000) in named weighted proportion to area (0.5),
real piece count (0.35), and the selected species' stem load (0.15), stepped to
£5k, floored at £150k. **Deliberately separate from `pricing.ts`** — tuning the
real cost model to hit a marketing number is the circular move `priceCopy.ts`'s
header refuses. `COMMISSION_DEMO_FIGURE` (`£150,000`) stays as the calibration
anchor and stays true. `useCountUp` (a pure `countUpValue` + a thin RAF hook,
plain DOM, outside the Canvas so the refs-not-state rule does not apply) tweens
it: 1100 ms as the bake settles, 550 ms on a species swap. `tabular-nums` on the
figure so the digits do not jitter mid-count.

## Left / for Daniel to decide

- **The figure often reads a flat £150,000 in practice, by design.** GRAMMAR caps
  footprint at 18 m² and the anchor is 14.3 m² / 194 pieces, so any draw at or
  below the anchor clamps to the £150k floor and species cannot move it down. It
  only visibly climbs on a larger-than-anchor draw (I saw 16.3 m² → £165–170k).
  If the demo needs to SHOW the number moving, either draw a large object, or
  lower the anchor so typical draws sit above it and animate. This is Sai's open
  question #1 and the team lead's "stays floored at £150,000" ruling; flagged so
  the flat reading is a choice, not a surprise on camera.
- **Seven swatch hues vs the house one-accent restraint** (Sai's open #2). They
  render as small informational dots and read clean in the baked frame, but it is
  a taste call for Daniel's eye. Hexes live in one map (`speciesSwatch.ts`),
  trivial to trim to the featured trio (Sai's open #3) if he wants — the row maps
  over `SPECIES`, so cutting is a filter, one line.
- **Chamfer + nut cap are subtle at demo distance.** The 3–4 mm arris and the
  Ø22 nut read on a close frame; at the turntable's default distance they are a
  refinement, not a headline. If Daniel wants them to read harder, the chamfer
  constants and `DOME_NUT_DIA_M` are one-liners.
- **Fasteners differ in colour only, not roughness.** `instanceColor` cannot vary
  roughness/metalness per instance, so the dark fasteners share the structural
  0.5/0.58 the visual pass tuned. The tone contrast carries the read (confirmed
  live). A fuller matte fastener would need a second instanced mesh — deferred,
  not needed for the demo.

## Gotcha earned here

**`instanceColor` alone does not tint a mesh in this three version.** The
fragment multiplies `diffuseColor` by `vColor` only under `USE_COLOR`, and
`USE_INSTANCING_COLOR` only initializes/multiplies `vColor` in the vertex stage.
So per-instance colour needs `material.vertexColors = true` AND a `color`
attribute on the base geometry — and `vertexColors` WITHOUT a `color` buffer
renders black (the missing attribute reads (0,0,0)). The working recipe is a
white `color` buffer on the base geometry + white `material.color` + per-instance
`setColorAt`. Read the shader chunk before trusting instanceColor lore.
